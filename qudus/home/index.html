About Petitions

Petitions

A petition is a formal appeal, entreaty, or plea to carry out a task or do something, it is usually directed or addressed to a governmental body, official or public establishment.


A petitioner is a person who pleads with a governmental institutionor body through a petition, by participating in it (signing it) in other for the desired action to be taken.
A petition management system is a software tool designed to handle and manage petitions.

Petition System (PS)

Online-PMS

This Petition System, uses a University as a case study, in which its students are able to to create awareness and sign petitions concerning issues they are not comfortable with or want to be changed, while keeping their identity a secret to prevent harassment or abuse from other parties. All petitions are then looked into and investigated by an impartial administrative body.

Students are required to read the Criteria & Procedures section below.


Petition Criteria & Procedures

Step 1

Students are required to create an account on the petition system. If you have already created an account, log in to your account.

Step 2

Students are required to fill out a form if you want to create a new petition. Petition titles should be short and explanatory, petition body should be well detailed with points (think quality not quantity). For more help on how to create good petitions, Click here

Step 3

Students can sign an already existing petition. A single petition can only be signed once by a student.

Step 4

An impartial internal administrative body carries out an investigation on a said petition to determine the neccessary action to be taken.

Step 5

The outcome of the investigation determines whether a petition will be declined or accepted. Petitions have 3 statuses, (Pending, Approved, Disapproved).
